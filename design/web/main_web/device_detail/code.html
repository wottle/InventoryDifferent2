Historical Significance

The Macintosh 128K, released in January 1984, was the first successful personal computer to feature a graphical user interface, built-in screen, and mouse. Its development began in the late 70s under Jef Raskin, eventually being taken over by Steve Jobs who envisioned it as an "appliance" computer for the masses.

Notable for its high-resolution 9-inch monochrome display and compact 'all-in-one' design, it shipped with two applications designed to show off its graphics: MacWrite and MacPaint. It was famously introduced with the "1984" Super Bowl commercial directed by Ridley Scott.
