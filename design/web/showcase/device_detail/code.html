Macintosh SE/30

The fastest of the classic compact Macs. A powerhouse in a portable frame, defining the pinnacle of monochrome computing.

Premium Collection Circa 1989

The Story

The Engine of the Desktop Publishing Revolution

Released in 1989, the Macintosh SE/30 remains the ultimate expression of the original all-in-one form factor. While it looked nearly identical to its predecessors, its internals told a vastly different story. Built around the powerful Motorola 68030 processor, it offered speeds that rivaled the high-end Macintosh IIx.

It was the first compact Mac to support high-density 1.44MB floppy disks and could be expanded to a then-staggering 128MB of RAM. For the professionals of the late 80s, this was the ultimate workstation.

This specific unit was sourced from a design studio in Basel, Switzerland, where it served as the primary typesetting machine for editorial layouts throughout the early 90s. The chassis retains its original texture with minimal UV discoloration—a rarity for this era.
